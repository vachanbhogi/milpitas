# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hackathon judges evaluating technical merit, and parents/teachers evaluating a phonics tool for pre-reading children. The page needs to impress on technical depth AND feel warm, safe, and playful.

## Product Purpose

Mumble is a voice-native phonics game for kids who can't read yet. Children speak into the mic, an alien named Zibi listens and learns, and the ship gets repaired one word at a time. Everything runs on-device — zero data leaves the browser.

## Brand Personality

Playful, bright, friendly. Bold colors, thick lines, cartoon warmth. Confident without being corporate, clever without being cold.

## Anti-references

Boring education app — beige, muted, textbook-y, clinical. The page should NOT look like a generic ed-tech product.

## Design Principles

1. **Sound-first, text-second** — The UI should feel alive with audio; text supports the experience, not the other way around.
2. **Playful confidence** — Bright colors, bold strokes, cartoon warmth. Never timid or washed out.
3. **Privacy is the product** — The on-device AI story IS the differentiator. Make it visible.
4. **For kids AND judges** — Delight children with the mascot and colors; impress adults with the architecture and clarity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High contrast text (4.5:1+). Screen reader friendly. The landing page itself is read aloud via speech synthesis for pre-readers navigating with parents.
